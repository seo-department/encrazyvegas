Basic Blackjack Strategy

There is a number of different blackjack strategies that you can learn to master the game. The house edge of a typical blackjack game is approximately 0.5%but this can change depending on your level of play and understanding of blackjack strategies.

Blackjack is a game based on luck and skills. That is why learning basic blackjack strategies can prove to be very useful to anyone who wants to make money from the game. Below we reveal the best blackjack strategies that will help improve your odds of winning real money more often.

Based on these charts, the probability of busting on a hand totaling 12 is still quite high, holding a one in three chance of losing. That is why it is important you hit or stand according to a basic strategy of blackjack.

Many think that just because they have a total of 12, 13, or 14, and even up to 16, they can always hit. This is exactly how you end up losing in the long run. To counter this, you must always weigh up your options by taking notice of the dealer’s up card and pitting it against your initial total. The basic strategy in blackjack is essentially a selection of concrete rules that have been derived from the two tables above.

Blackjack Strategy – Comprehensive Summary

Not everyone can learn blackjack strategies using charts. That is why we have put together a summary of all the strategy charts you can find. Once you are able to recite these strategies, you will be ready to hit any blackjack table.

Surrendering

  • Player 16 surrenders against a dealer’s 9 through Ace; otherwise don’t surrender and revert back to hard totals.
  • Player 15 surrenders against a dealer’s 10; otherwise don’t surrender and revert back to hard totals.

Splitting

  • Always split aces.
  • Never split tens.
  • Split a pair of 9’s against dealer 2 through 9, except for 7, otherwise stand.
  • Always split 8’s
  • Split a pair of 7’s against dealer 2 through 7, otherwise hit.
  • Split a pair of 6’s against dealer 2 through 6, otherwise hit.
  • Double a pair of 5’s against dealer 2 through 9 otherwise hit.
  • Split a pair of 4’s against dealer 5 and 6 , otherwise hit.
  • Split a pair of 3’s against dealer 2 through 7, otherwise hit.
  • Split a pair of 2’s against dealer 2 through 7, otherwise hit.

Soft totals 

A soft total is any hand that has an Ace, which counts as 11 to start.

  • Soft hand of 20 always stands
  • Soft hand of 19 doubles against dealer 6, otherwise stand.
  • Soft hand of 18 doubles against dealer 2 through 6, and hits against 9 through Ace, otherwise stand.
  • Soft hand of 13 through 17 doubles against dealer 3 through 6, otherwise hit.
  • Soft hand of 15 and 16 doubles against dealer 4 through 6, otherwise hit.
  • Soft hand of 14 doubles against dealer 5 through 6, otherwise hit.
