How to Play Blackjack

Before we get into the rules and gameplay of blackjack, first you need to understand how a round of blackjack is played. The aim of the game is simple. However, it may be complicated when it comes to choosing how to play out your round to obtain your final blackjack hand. Players and dealers compete to get a hand as close to 21 as possible without going bust. Going bust refers to adding cards to your hand that give you a total exceeding 21.

To help beginner blackjack players. Here is a step by step guide of how blackjack works:

Step one

Players must use the different coloured chips to place their bets on the blackjack table. Each of the coloured chips, hold a different value.

Step two

Every player, including the dealer, then gets dealt two cards. Players receive both their cards face up, while dealers receive one card face up and one face down. However, the way cards are dealt may vary with the different blackjack variants.

Step three

After everyone has received their cards, the dealer will check for blackjack. While the dealer, checks for blackjack you can look at your cards to figure out how to play out the round. Players can either select to hit (receive another card) or stand. If the player opts for another card, they stand when they are satisfied with their hand total.

Step four

The dealer now reveals their face down card and also has the option to hit or stand. Dealers are obliged to hit in any instance when their blackjack hand is less than the players.

Step five

The round ends with the dealer calculating everyone’s final hand. The person closest to 21 or equal to 21 wins. Hand totals that bust automatically results in a loss.

Glossary for Online Blackjack

  • Bankroll: The amount of money you’ve dedicated to your casino account to bet with.
  • Bust: This refers to a hand where the total exceeds 21.
  • Hit: When a player requests another card from the dealer.
  • House edge: The house edge refers to the mathematical calculation of the advantage the casino has over a player.
  • Late Surrender: A side bet available to players in certain blackjack variants. When players do not think they have a sufficient hand to win, they will place this bet after the dealer has checked for blackjack. Players surrender half their bet rather than risk losing their entire original bet.
  • Natural: When a player or dealer has an initial hand made up of any 10 and an Ace.
  • Push: This is another term for a tie. Your original bet will be returned to you in this instance.
