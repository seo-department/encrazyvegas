2. Splitting Pairs

Regardless of the variants, you are playing or the up-card of the dealer, if you have a pair of 8’s or Aces, you can’t go wrong with splitting. Splitting 8’s and Aces gives the upper hands as they are now playing two hands. When splitting the 8s, each hand will start with an 8. This is better than starting the hand of 16 and hitting. Players are less likely to lose more money. Depending on the dealer’s upper card, players will either cut their losses or win more.
