Sports Betting Games

There are four core sporting events to bet on: NFL, NHL, NBA, and the MLB. These are followed by other major events which draws the attention of bettors.

Sports Betting NFL

The National Football League (NFL) is a Pro American football league which has 32 teams. The NFL season consists of teams from the National Football Conference (NFC) and American Football Conference (NFC). It forms as one of the major sporting leagues in North America. NFL games also feature the highest level of professional football.

Sports Betting NBA

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is the professional basketball league. There are two conferences which make up the league, the Western Conference and Eastern Conference.

Each conference has 15 teams, split into three divisions. The top eight teams from each conference advance to play each other. The team standing is crowned the champions of the season.

Sports Betting NHL

The National Hockey League (NHL) is a pro ice hockey league in North America. There are 24 teams from the USA and seven teams from Canada that compete. It is the premier ice hockey league of the world.

Sports Betting MLB

The Major League Baseball (MLB) is another core sports betting category that attracts bettors. It is one of the oldest major professional baseball sports leagues in America.

Between 1879 and 1901 the National League and American League competed as two separate legal entities. From 1903, they cooperated but legally remained separate entities. However, in the 2000s they merged to form the MLB.

Sports Betting Glossary

Accumulator: A series of bets with one wager – similar to the parlay.

Arbitrage: A betting strategy where one wager covers all possible outcomes of an event.

Beard: When someone else places a bet on behalf of another person who wishes to remain anonymous.

Bonus: Many sportsbook operators offer a financial bonus to players for various reasons. The most common bonus is a sign-up bonus.

Chalk: A term used to describe the team is the favorite to win.

Opening Line: The first point spread available for a game.

Closing Line: Where the point spread is when the game begins.

Closing Line Value: Used by bettors to measure the value of the line they wagered before the line that was the last available before a game begins.

Contrarian: Betting against the trends of the mainstream popular opinions.

Dead Heat: Another way to say tie result.

Dog: Anther way to say underdog - the team that is not expected to win. Also referred to as a puppy.

Early Cash Out: When bettor settle for a certain dollar amount before the event is over. 

Flat Betting: All bet amounts are the same. Bettors do not change their bet amounts, whether they are winning or losing; they keep it the same for each round.

Handicapper: Person who analyses sport events to predict the winner.

Kelly Criterion: A type of bankroll management strategy for someone who seeks to limit losses while maximizing wins.

Middle/Middling: Playing different sides of the same game to give bettors multiple chances to win on the same game.

Novelty Bet: Placing a bet on a sports book that is not sport-related. For example, placing a bet on the Oscars.

Off the Board: When it is uncertain a sporting event will go ahead, or a player will not be able to participate, sportsbooks remove the game odds from the betting board.

Round Robin: Placing multiple parlay bets at the same time.

Tout: Selling or giving away sports betting picks by a person.

What does 5 to 1 odds pay?

In basic terms it means, if you place a bet of $10, you will get paid $50 for a winning bet. In other words, you will get five time the amount wagered back in winnings.

What does point spread mean?

Point spread is one of the most popular betting options in college football and NFL. It is a figure set by the oddsmakers that serves as the margin of victory. The team you bet on has to either cover or beat the set margin for your bet to win.
