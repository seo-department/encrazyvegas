How to Play Bonus Poker

Bonus poker is based on the Jacks or Better variation with a similar game play and rules. Each round starts with placing money into the bonus poker machine (depositing into your casino account). You will then decide how many coins you would like to bet. You can bet up to 5 coins per round.

You will then receive five cards from the computer. You will be allowed to discard between 0 and 5 of the cards. Any cards you discard, you will receive new ones. This will then become your final hand, and will receive a payout based on the pay table.

You will need at least a pair of Jacks to receive a payout. The highest paying combination is a royal flush. There is also a bonus payout for a four-of-a-kind. But this will depend on the rank of the four of a kind.

Bonus Poker Hand Combinations

  • Royal Flush: The highest paying winning combination consisting of an Ace, King, Queen, Jack, and 10 of the same suit. You should always bet the maximum amount; otherwise, it can significantly reduce the payout for a royal flush.
  • Straight Flush: A hand consisting of 5 cards of the same suit in consecutive order. The payout for a straight flush and all other lower ranks increase steadily with different coins. It is only a royal flush where the difference is big.
  • Four of a Kind: A hand that contains four cards of the same value. In bonus, poker there are different payouts for the different four of a kind ranks.
  • Full House: A hand made up of a three of a kind and pair. This is an important hand as the payouts change depending on the machine you are playing. The 8/5 bonus poker version has the highest payout for a full house.
  • Flush: A five-card hand that contains cards from different suits. Another significant hand that can also change depending on the machine you are playing. In bonus poker 8/5, you can receive a bonus payout of 5:1 for this hand.
  • Straight: A hand consisting of 5 consecutive numbers, but of different suits.
  • Three of a Kind: A hand containing three cards of the same value.
  • Two Pair: A hand that consists of two separate pairs of cards.

Bonus Poker Strategy

You should hold the following cards:

  • Four cards to a royal flush
  • Four of a kind
  • Three of a kind, straight, flush, full house
  • Four straight card flushes
  • Four card flushes
  • Three card royal flushes
  • Four card straights
  • Inside straights with three or more high cards
  • Low pairs for three of a kind
  • High card pairs

Bonus Poker Deluxe

There are different bonus poker games. One of the other popular versions is bonus poker deluxe is another variant of bonus poker. In this game, there is only one payout for a four of a kind. There Is also a lower payout for two pairs. For 2 pairs, you will receive an even money payout, instead of the original 2:1. There are also adjusted payouts for a full house and flush.
