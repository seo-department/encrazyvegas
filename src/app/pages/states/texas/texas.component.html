History of Gambling in Texas

The gambling in Texas dates back to before Texas became a state. The early settlers enjoyed casinos, horse racing, and lotteries. In the first constitution of Texas, all forms of gambling were banned. It meant betting on games of chance, horse racing, lotteries, or any other form of gambling that was thought of at the time was banned.

1933: Pari-Mutuel Betting Legalized  

However, in 1933, the first-breakthrough appeared. It was when pari-mutuel betting on horse racing was legalized. It only lasted four years.

1989: Horse Race Betting Returns

Horse race betting returned to the face track of Texas. No additional forms of gambling have since been allowed at these venues. In neighboring states, some horse racing venues have slot machines for players to gamble on.

Fight Against Casino Gambling

On two separate fronts, Texas has had to fight off casino gambling. The first from tribes who took claims to federal courts. This came after authorities shut down their high-stakes bingo halls and casino developments. Texas has since won the arguments, and today there is only one tribal casino operating – Kickapoo Lucky Eagle.

The second front was for casinos on water, with boat casinos from international waters offering their selection of games. A law from 1948 was used by the Texas Government to fight off boat casinos. They required them to be afloat for a minimum of 24 hours and to have docked at a foreign port.

While the Texas Government may have won their fights against casinos, many residents of Texas choose to cross into nearby states such as Louisiana and Oklahoma, where casinos are more conveniently located.
