Probability vs Casino Games with Best Odds

Probability refers to the number of times an outcome occurs versus the number of times the experiment is conducted over the long run. Odds on the other hand look at the number of times an outcome does not occur compared to the number of times it does occur. For example, in a card casino game, if a card is randomly selected from a standard deck of 52 cards, the probability is one in four. The odds for this are three to one. When looking at the true odds, this is represented by the payoff that will make the bet on an event fair. For example, a bet on a single number in double-zero roulette has a probability of 1 in 38.
