History of MasterCard

The origin of MasterCard dates back to as far as the 1940s. During this period, several U.S banks gave specially-issued papers to their customers. These specially-issued papers could be used like cash in stores. Over the next decade, several franchises evolved in which one bank in a city or state will accept these cards as payments. However, only specific merchants accepted this.

1966, the Interbank Card Association (ICA) was formed. Later this became known as MasterCard. They also established rules for authorization, clearing, and settlements. ICA was also responsible for handling the marketing, security, and legal aspects of running the MasterCard organization.

How MasterCard was Formed

1968, the ICA formed a global network. This was done by creating an association with Banco Nacional in Mexico. In the same year, they formed an alliance in Europe with Eurocard, and the first Japanese members also came onboard.

1970, members from Africa and Australia joined ICA. With international growth, the ICA changed its name to MasterCard international.

1980, MasterCard expanded to Asia and Latin America.

1985, Cirrus Atm network acquired. Also, there was the launch of Maestro.  

In 1987, MasterCard became the first payment card to be used in China.

1988, the first MasterCard card was issued in the Soviet Union.

1991, Maestro saw the world’s first online point-of-sale debit network was launched.

2002, MasterCard merged with Europay international. They also changed from a membership association to a private share corporation.  The private share corporation changed prepared MasterCard for its public offering in 2006.

2009 to 2012, Acquisitions were designed to assist the focus of innovations. Some of these innovations include Orbiscom.

In 2010, Orbiscom changed to MasterCard Labs.

2013, Provus was acquired by Provus. They are a processor-based in Turkey. MasterCard also started to extend to more than 210 countries.

In 2016, MasterCard changed its brand identity.

2019, MasterCard changed its logo and removed their name from the logo.
