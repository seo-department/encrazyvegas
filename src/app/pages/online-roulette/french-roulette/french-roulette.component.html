French Roulette vs. American

French roulette is different from other roulette variants because of the unique table features. French roulette also has a lower house edge compared to other variants such as American roulette. The additional gameplay rule is the reason for the reduced house edge.

French roulette is played with a standard European roulette wheel with 37 compartments. These are numbered from 1 to 36, alternating in colors red and black and 0 (zero), which appears in green.

In comparison, the American roulette wheel has 38 pockets. Numbers 1 to 36, alternating in colors red and black and two green pockets – 0 and 00. The American roulette wheel has a double zero.

Another differentiation between the two is the positioning of the roulette wheels. They are both located differently.

Another distinct difference you will notice is the position of the outside betting fields. On the American roulette table, the outside bets are found on the left side. While on the French roulette table, the outside bets are split and located on either side of the table layout. These bets may sometimes be translated to English, which you will be able to see the French name below it.

French Roulette Rules

French roulette follows the same roulette wheel and rules as the European game. But, there are a few additional rules when the zero comes in to play. When the ball lands in the zero pocket, not all even money bets are lost. Instead, one of two rules apply:

  1. En Prison Rule: When you translate “En Prison,” it means in prison. With this rule, when the ball lands on zero, your bet is not lost. Instead, it will stay remain there for the next round. However, if the ball lands on the zero pocket for consecutive spins, the bet is lost.
  2. La Partage Rule: When you translate “La Partage,” it means splitting. With this rule, when the ball lands on the zero number, you will lose only half of your bet.
